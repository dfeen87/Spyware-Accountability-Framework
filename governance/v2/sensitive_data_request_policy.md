# Sensitive Data Request Policy

The Spyware Accountability Framework (SAF) public repository strictly prohibits the inclusion of real, actionable IOCs, exploit signatures, or PII. We use purely synthetic datasets for development and demonstration.

However, the framework is designed to be used with real data in private environments. Occasionally, vetted partners (NGOs, journalists) may need access to validated, non-synthetic intelligence or custom rule sets that are too sensitive for public release.

## 1. Eligibility
To request access to sensitive data or custom rulesets maintained privately by the SAF maintainers, the requester must be:
- An established NGO focused on digital rights or human rights.
- A recognized investigative journalist.
- A vetted security researcher with a track record of defensive publication.

**Law enforcement, intelligence agencies, and commercial vendors are categorically ineligible.**

## 2. Request Process
1. The requester submits a private inquiry via email to the Governance Board.
2. The request must include:
    - The organization's mission and history.
    - The specific purpose of the request (e.g., "Investigating targeting of civil society in Region X").
    - A guarantee that the data will be used defensively and not to target individuals or improve offensive capabilities.
3. The Governance Board reviews the request.

## 3. Approval & Dissemination
If approved by a majority of the Governance Board:
- Data is shared via secure, encrypted channels.
- The recipient agrees to a non-disclosure agreement regarding specific technical indicators that could burn ongoing investigations, while maintaining the right to publish overarching findings.
- The Governance Board maintains a private registry of approved requests for auditing purposes.
