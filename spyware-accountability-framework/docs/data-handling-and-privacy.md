# Data Handling and Privacy Policy

The Spyware Accountability Framework processes highly sensitive information regarding advanced, state-grade, and mercenary spyware infrastructure. However, our primary goal is to **defend human rights and protect vulnerable communities**. This goal necessitates stringent data handling and privacy protocols.

## 🚫 No Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

The most critical rule of this repository:
**WE DO NOT ACCEPT, PROCESS, OR STORE ANY PII OF SPYWARE VICTIMS OR TARGETS.**

This includes:
- Email addresses
- Phone numbers
- Names, locations, or affiliations
- Full, unredacted URLs containing target-specific tokens or parameters
- Device identifiers (IMEIs, MAC addresses) belonging to victims

Any dataset, packet capture, log file, or document submitted to this repository must be entirely stripped of such information. If you discover PII within our examples, rulesets, or tests, please immediately contact our security team as detailed in our [Security Policy](../SECURITY.md) rather than opening a public issue.

## 🗄️ Synthetic Data Strategy

To develop, test, and demonstrate the capabilities of our pipelines and AILEE integrations without risking the exposure of sensitive data or tipping off adversaries to ongoing investigations, we rely exclusively on **synthetic data**.

- All tests within the `tests/` directory must use mock, synthetic inputs.
- All demonstrations within the `examples/` directory must use fake vendor names, fake domains, and fake IP spaces (e.g., `192.0.2.0/24`, `198.51.100.0/24`, `203.0.113.0/24` as per RFC 5737).
- Example rulesets (YARA, Sigma, Suricata) must be clearly marked as synthetic and demonstrate structural concepts rather than provide live, actionable indicators of compromise (IOCs).

## 🛡️ Analytical Data Handling

When using the Spyware Accountability Framework locally to process real-world data (e.g., a packet capture from a compromised device, or a list of suspected mercenary domains):

1. **Local Execution:** We strongly recommend running all pipelines locally, within an isolated analysis environment (e.g., an offline VM, a dedicated analysis machine, or a secure container).
2. **Minimal Data Retention:** Once analysis is complete and reports are generated, secure the original data. Do not leave sensitive packet captures or raw logs lying around unencrypted.
3. **Redaction Before Sharing:** If you intend to share the outputs (e.g., the JSON reports, graphs, or IOC lists generated by the pipelines) with the broader research community or NGOs, ensure that you manually review and redact any target-specific parameters that the pipelines may not have caught.
4. **Third-Party Integrations:** Be aware of the data flow when integrating the `ailee_core` interfaces with external AI/ML services (e.g., cloud-based LLMs for OSINT classification). Do not send raw, sensitive data to third-party APIs without a clear understanding of their data retention policies and the risks involved. We strongly encourage the use of local, self-hosted models for sensitive analysis whenever possible.
